# Why This

## The thing I noticed

I was going through my Instamart history one afternoon and realized I could roughly reconstruct the last few months of my life from it. Busy weeks showed up as gaps — zero grocery orders, lots of restaurant delivery. Good stretches showed up as regular Instamart hauls: onions, tomatoes, dal, the usual.

The data was already there. Swiggy had it. I just had no way to look at it in a way that meant anything.

Most "order history" features just show you a list. Sorted by date, maybe filterable by restaurant. That's not insight — that's a receipt archive.

## Why existing tools don't solve this

I looked around. There are apps that pull your Zomato or Swiggy food orders and show you spend charts. They're fine for tracking how much you spent. They don't tell you anything about *why* you ordered, or what the pattern means.

The reason is they only have one vertical. Restaurant orders, or grocery orders — not both together.

That's actually the interesting thing about Swiggy as a platform. They have both sides. Delivery and grocery and dine-out, all tied to the same account. That combination tells a story that neither vertical alone can tell.

## What I'm actually trying to build

Not an analytics app. Not a chatbot. Something closer to a mirror — one that shows you your food behavior in a way that's immediately recognizable.

When I showed the food mode concept to a few friends, the reaction wasn't "that's a cool feature." It was "wait, yeah, I do that." That's the feeling I want to build toward.

The technical challenge is interesting too: getting an AI agent to reason across three MCP tool sets simultaneously and produce insights that are grounded in real data — not vibes, not averages, but things that are actually true about your specific orders.

That's what I'm building.

# User Stories

Real scenarios SwiggyLens is built to handle. These aren't personas from a product doc — they're patterns that show up in actual Swiggy data.

---

## The person who doesn't realize they've stopped cooking

Rahul used to cook 3-4 times a week. He'd do a big Instamart haul on Sundays — vegetables, pulses, the works. Over the last 6 weeks, the hauls got smaller and less frequent. He's been ordering delivery almost every day. He hasn't thought about it much.

SwiggyLens opens and tells him: *"You've been in Ordering Mode for 6 weeks. Instamart orders are down 80% from your usual. Before that, you were consistently in Cooking Mode."*

He didn't ask for that. He didn't know he needed it. But it's immediately true and recognizable.

---

## The person who wants to understand their actual food spend

Priya tracks her expenses but food feels murky — there's delivery, there's groceries, there's the occasional dinner out. She knows she's spending "a lot" but doesn't have a clear number.

She asks SwiggyLens: *"How much did I actually spend on food last month across everything?"*

It pulls delivery orders, Instamart orders, and dine-out bills from Swiggy — gives her a single number broken down by vertical. She finds out Instamart is actually 40% of her food spend, not the small percentage she assumed.

---

## The person figuring out their food patterns for health reasons

Aditya is trying to eat better. He's been making an effort to cook more and order less. He wants to know if it's actually working.

He opens SwiggyLens on a Sunday evening: *"You've placed 4 Instamart orders in the last 2 weeks — up from your usual 1-2. Delivery frequency is down 45% compared to last month. You're in Cooking Mode."*

He didn't have to calculate anything. The app just reflected what he'd been doing back to him.

---

## The person who goes out more than they realize

Neha doesn't think of herself as someone who dines out a lot. But she's been having more catch-ups with friends lately.

SwiggyLens: *"4 dine-in occasions this month — your highest since January. Average spend per occasion: ₹1,800. All on weekends. Social Mode active."*

It's a small thing but it's accurate. She recognizes it immediately.

---

## The person who just wants to ask a specific question

Someone wants to know: *"What's the most I've ever spent on a single Swiggy order?"* Or: *"How many times have I ordered from that one biryani place?"* Or: *"What did I order during that week in March when I was travelling?"*

These are questions no existing Swiggy feature answers easily. With SwiggyLens, you just ask.
